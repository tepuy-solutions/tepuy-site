Published: July 2025 · Tepuy Solutions Research

Should Australians invest in real estate or global shares? We've analysed more than 50 years of data to answer that question. From property prices and wages to immigration and interest rates, this deep dive reveals the trends behind the headlines.

Housing Price Growth vs Wage Growth (1990–2024)

Using verified data from ABS and CoreLogic, we compare annual growth in Australian housing prices and wages over 35 years. Housing prices have consistently outpaced wage growth, contributing to reduced affordability.

Migration Pressure vs Housing Supply

Net overseas migration has surged since the early 2000s. When dwelling completions lag population growth, property prices rise. The gap between migration and housing construction is critical to understand.

Population growth has consistently outpaced new housing supply in Australia.

Interest Rates and Property Cycles

Interest rates shape borrowing capacity. Lower rates often drive asset booms. Australia's recent price surges have coincided with rate cuts and ultra-cheap credit.

Property price booms often follow interest rate cuts.

S&P 500 in AUD vs Australian Property

When measured in AUD and with dividends reinvested, the S&P 500 has outpaced Australian property over the long term. It's more volatile but also more diversified, liquid, and scalable.

The S&P 500 has delivered strong inflation-beating returns in AUD over 30+ years.

Risks and Tax Considerations

  • Property: High entry/exit costs, leverage risk, maintenance, low liquidity. Tax varies by structure and state rules.
  • S&P 500: Market volatility, currency risk, CGT on foreign ETFs, lower control. However, low fees, easy diversification, and better compounding potential.
